Dublin Dumpster Permit Requirements: What You Need to Know

One of the most common questions we receive from Dublin homeowners is: "Do I need a permit for my dumpster rental?"

When You DON'T Need a Permit in Dublin

The good news is that most residential dumpster rentals in Dublin don't require a permit if:

  • The dumpster is placed entirely on your private property (driveway, yard, etc.)
  • It doesn't block sidewalks or public right-of-way
  • It doesn't obstruct traffic visibility or safety
  • It's not placed in the street or on the curb

When You DO Need a Permit in Dublin

You'll need to obtain a permit from the City of Dublin if:

  • The dumpster must be placed in the street or on a public right-of-way
  • Your driveway can't accommodate the dumpster
  • You're blocking any portion of the sidewalk

How to Get a Dublin Dumpster Permit:

  1. Contact the Dublin Division of Engineering at (614) 410-4600
  2. Provide your project address and estimated dumpster placement dates
  3. Pay the permit fee (typically $50-100 for residential projects)
  4. Display the permit visibly on or near the dumpster

Best Practices for Dumpster Placement in Dublin Neighborhoods

Proper dumpster placement protects your property and keeps your neighbors happy. Here are our top recommendations for Dublin residents:

Protect Your Driveway

Dublin homes often feature decorative concrete, stamped driveways, or paver stones. To prevent damage:

  • Request plywood boards under the dumpster wheels (we provide these at no extra charge)
  • Avoid placing the dumpster on areas with underground utilities
  • Keep the dumpster away from irrigation systems
  • Don't overfill the dumpster, which adds weight and increases damage risk

Consider Your Neighbors

Dublin's close-knit communities appreciate consideration. When placing your dumpster:

  • Position it to minimize visual impact from neighboring properties
  • Avoid blocking shared driveways or mailboxes
  • Keep noise to a minimum during early mornings and late evenings when loading
  • Inform immediate neighbors about the dumpster's arrival and pickup schedule

Accessibility for Delivery and Pickup

Our delivery trucks need adequate space to safely place and remove your dumpster:

  • Ensure 60 feet of clearance for truck approach (about 4 car lengths)
  • Provide at least 23 feet of vertical clearance (watch for overhead power lines or tree branches)
  • Clear the area of vehicles, basketball hoops, or other obstacles
  • Unlock any gates if the dumpster will be placed in a backyard area

What You Can (and Can't) Put in Your Dublin Dumpster

Understanding what's allowed in your dumpster rental helps you stay compliant with local regulations and avoid additional fees.

Acceptable Materials

  • Household junk and furniture
  • Construction debris (wood, drywall, concrete, brick)
  • Roofing shingles and materials
  • Flooring and carpeting
  • Yard waste (branches, leaves, grass clippings)
  • Appliances (some restrictions apply)
  • Metal and scrap materials

Prohibited Materials

For safety and environmental reasons, the following items cannot be placed in dumpsters:

  • Hazardous waste (paint, chemicals, asbestos)
  • Automotive fluids (oil, antifreeze, gasoline)
  • Batteries (car batteries, lithium batteries)
  • Tires
  • Electronics with cathode ray tubes (old TVs, monitors)
  • Medical waste
  • Propane tanks

Proper Disposal Alternatives: Dublin residents can dispose of hazardous materials at the Solid Waste Authority of Central Ohio (SWACO) household hazardous waste collection events. Visit SWACO's website for collection dates and locations.
